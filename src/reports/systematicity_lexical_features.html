Systematicity across grammatical category and other lexical features

Introduction

This report investigates whether the sub-morphemic form-meaning systematicity of different words correlates with various lexical features, including grammatical category, age of acquisition, and word frequency.

Previous work (Monaghan et al, 2014) has found that age of acquisition (AoA) predicts systematicity, above and beyond other variables like orthographic/phonological length. Specifically, earlier-acquired words were more systematic.

We calculated the systematicity of each word following the same procedure as Monaghan et al, 2014. The Jupyter notebook containing this analysis can be found here. We obtained monosyllabic, monomorphemic words from CELEX, then filtered on the words that appeared in the Google News word embeddings, resulting in 2082 words overall.

We then calculated a global correlation score between the form and meaning distances of each wordpair. To obtain by-word systematicity scores, we compared this global correlation to the correlation coefficient when omitting a particular word: the difference in scores is deemed the contribution a given word makes to a lexicon’s global systematicity.

As Monaghan et al 2014 do, we remove words with more than one word class:

word_classes$duplicate = duplicated(word_classes$Word)

duplicate_words = filter(word_classes, duplicate==TRUE)$Word
## Warning: package 'bindrcpp' was built under R version 3.4.4
word_classes_filtered = filter(word_classes, (Word %in% duplicate_words) == FALSE)

Combining all together

Finally, we merged each of these datasets together:

merged = merge(word_systematicity_scores, aoa_kuperman, on="word")
merged_all = merge(merged, distinct(word_classes_filtered), on="word")

This results in only 784 observations.

Results

Our chief question is whether the systematicity of a word is related to its:

  • Grammatical Class
  • Age of Acquisition
  • Frequency
  • Length

To answer this, we regressed systematicity in a standard OLS regression against each of those variables:

model_omnibus = lm(data = merged_all,
                   impact ~ Rating.Mean + Class + CobLog + word_length)
summary(model_omnibus)

Next Steps
